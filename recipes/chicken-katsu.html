Chicken Katsu

Description

Chicken Katsu is a Japanese dish made with a breaded and fried chicken cutlet. It has a crunchy golden crust on the outside and a juicy, tender center. Often served with shredded cabbage, steamed rice, and katsu sauce, it is a comfort food favorite in both restaurants and home kitchens

This dish teaches key cooking steps like breading and frying. Although it looks fancy, it’s easy to make with basic ingredients. You can enjoy Chicken Katsu on its own, in a sandwich, or as part of a rice bowl. Its crispy texture and balanced flavor make it a standout.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts (pounded flat)
  • Salt & pepper
  • ½ cup flour
  • 1 egg (beaten)
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • Oil for frying
  • Tonkatsu sauce (optional)

Steps

  1. Season chicken with salt and pepper.
  2. Dredge in flour, dip in egg, coat in breadcrumbs.
  3. Shallow-fry until golden and cooked through.
  4. Drain on paper towel and serve with sauce.
